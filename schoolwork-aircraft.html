Aircraft Design Project

MATLABSimulinkXFLR5Aircraft performanceFlight controlsTechnical documentation

The goal of this project was to develop a multi-functional agricultural aircraft, with autonomous functionality when dusting crops, as well as with space for manual piloting during ferry missions. Throughout the semester, the project progressed from a requirements-driven Conceptual Design Report (mid-semester) to a final aircraft model and performance evaluation in a Final Design Report. My work focused on sizing and aerodynamic decisions early, then on building an aerodynamic dataset using XFLR5 and integrating it into a flight dynamics/control simulation for maneuver performance using Simulink.

Conceptual Design Report

Design Approach

I documented a requirements-driven approach that connects mission needs (payload, flight profile, and operational constraints) to a feasible baseline configuration. The goal was to make early decisions traceable: every geometry or sizing choice had a performance or constraint-based reason behind it.

Initial Sizing (W/S + Weight Iteration)

I performed the first-pass sizing using wing loading as the primary driver, then iterated fuel fraction, empty weight fraction, and gross weight estimation to converge on a consistent design point. This included trade study logic to understand how sizing choices moved the aircraft through the feasible design space.

Aerodynamics (Airfoil + Wing Sizing)

I evaluated candidate airfoils using performance-relevant metrics and summarized the decision in a weighted selection matrix. The chosen airfoil and wing sizing were then carried forward as the aerodynamic baseline for the final configuration and later simulation work.

XFLR5 Data → MATLAB Processing

I developed MATLAB scripts to extract XFLR5 results and compute coefficient increments (e.g., changes due to control deflection or other configuration effects). These processed outputs were packaged into a form suitable for lookup/interpolation inside the simulation environment.

Simulink Control & Maneuver Evaluation

I integrated the processed aerodynamic data into a Simulink-based flight model and used it to evaluate aircraft behavior in multiple scenarios. The final analysis emphasized stability and control behavior under representative maneuvers and flight phases.

Performance Cases

  • Glide: The first performance parameter was evaluating the aircraft's trimmed glide behavior, focusing on longitudinal stability and phugoid damping. This simulation was used to verify steady-state glide speed, elevator trim requirements, and effective lift-to-drag performance once settled.
  • Climb: The second performance parameter was evlauating the climb capability using cooridnated thrust and elivator control to transition from level flight into a controlled ascent. I took care to remain within elevator deflection and available thrust limits, along with meeting climb rate requirements and remaining within reasonable limits on angle of attack.
  • Hold: The thrid performance parameter was simulating a sustained holding pattern to evaluate lateral-directional stability and control during coordinated turns. This was achieved through a combination of roll, pitch, and yaw control, ensuring minimal altitude loss and coordinating sideslip angles throughout the maneuver.
  • Landing: The final performance parameter was modeling a full landing pattern combining longitudinal and lateral control, including descending turns and a stabilized final approach. This maneuver was used as a verification of the full control system, bringing together the descent, turn, and hold performances into a single continuous scenario. Rather than focusing on precise landing distance metrics, the simulation emphasized controllability, trim behavior, and coordination throughout the approach sequence, serving as a validation of the aircraft’s modeled flight dynamics and control logic.

GPS Positioning Testing

At the same time I was completing this course, I was also taking Introduction to Navigation Systems, which got me thinking about how GPS positioning would work for an aircraft like this. So, I developed an MATLAB code to express a short GPS positioning representation of the aircraft's final approach flight path, using satellite position estimation concepts from my concurrent Navigation coursework.

While I do admit that this is a completely arbitrary and user-controlled method of estimating position, I included this in my report as a test of what I learned and understood about Navigation, as well as an exploration of the applications of GPS positioning in the Aerospace field.
