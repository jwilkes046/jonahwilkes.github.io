Overview

As an Astrophysics Researcher at Illinois Tech, I have been leading a project to examine the formation mechanisms and classification of Sub-Subgiant stars. I have analyzed stellar spectra of 98 systems to extract radial velocities and orbital parameters of each stellar source. To complement this observational data, I built Monte Carlo simulations of synthetic binary populations, with which I was able to directly compare the modeled distributions to the observed data.

I am currently writing this work into a publishable document, focusing on the interpretation of the observational data and statistical results. My analysis indicates that Sub-Subgiant stars are very likely to be binary systems, reinforcing binarity as a fundamental characteristic of this stellar population. My efforts toward this publication include synthesizing these results within existing theoretical formation channels and developing a consistent interpretation of the observed orbital properties. This paper aims to present a statistically grounded classification of Sub-Subgiant systems and their dominant formation mechanisms.
