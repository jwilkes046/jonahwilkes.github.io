Overview

I am an Aerospace Engineering student at Illinois Tech with a strong academic focus on dynamics, simulation, and systems-level analysis. Through a series of semester-long, project-based courses, I have developed experience applying theoretical concepts in aerodynamics, astrodynamics, and navigation to open-ended engineering problems. The work highlighted below represents three major projects from Aircraft Design, Spacecraft Dynamics and Design, and Introduction to Navigation Systems, each emphasizing the translation of fundamental principles into practical modeling, analysis, and design decisions. Together, these projects reflect my interest in understanding how complex aerospace systems are modeled, simulated, and evaluated across multiple domains.

Highlights

Aircraft Design

In my Aircraft Design coursework at Illinois Tech, I led the conceptual and preliminary design of a fixed-wing aircraft, taking primary responsibility for the aerodynamic configuration, performance analysis, and control development. Working within a small team, I guided early design decisions including overall sizing, wing planform definition, and airfoil selection, translating high-level mission requirements into a viable aircraft configuration. Later in the semester, I conducted detailed aerodynamic analysis using XFLR5, developed custom MATLAB scripts to extract and process aerodynamic data, and integrated these results into a Simulink-based flight control model. I evaluated aircraft performance and stability across multiple flight conditions and maneuvers, gaining experience connecting aerodynamic design, dynamic modeling, and control system behavior in a cohesive aircraft analysis.

Spacecraft Dynamics and Design

My coursework in Spacecraft Dynamics and Spacecraft Design provided a two-semester progression from orbital mechanics fundamentals to full mission systems engineering. In Spacecraft Dynamics, I designed and simulated interplanetary trajectories for a multi-flyby mission involving ten of Jupiter’s moons, developing numerical trajectory models from first principles using Python and the REBOUND N-body simulation framework. This work involved constructing three-dimensional, time-resolved spacecraft trajectories and evaluating orbital behavior across multiple mission phases. In the subsequent Spacecraft Design course, I applied these astrodynamics methods within a broader systems-engineering context while contributing to the design of an unmanned mission to Saturn’s moon Titan. I led trajectory design efforts while also collaborating on spacecraft architecture, payload deployment concepts, and surface exploration systems, including deployable satellites and a submersible vehicle intended for Titan’s methane lakes. Together, these projects emphasized the integration of orbital mechanics, numerical simulation, and systems-level mission design.

Navigation Systems

My coursework in Introduction to Navigation Systems, supported by my Optimal State Estimation course, established a strong foundation in modern positioning and estimation theory. Through lecture material and a cumulative project, I developed a working understanding of satellite-based navigation systems, including GNSS positioning, differential GPS techniques, and augmentation methods. I applied state-space modeling and Kalman filtering to estimate position and velocity in the presence of measurement noise and uncertainty, gaining familiarity with how navigation solutions are constructed and evaluated. The course emphasized estimation structure, error sources, and solution robustness, providing conceptual grounding that has informed my understanding of navigation and signal processing research.
