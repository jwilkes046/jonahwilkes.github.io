Overview

As an Aerospace Researcher at Illinois Tech, I am currently conducting research in Global Navigation Satellite System Reflectometry (GNSS-R), focusing on the processing and analysis of reflected GNSS signals collected over Antarctic glacial surfaces. Working with raw radio-frequency data acquired using a USRP-based receiver, I am developing MATLAB-based signal processing algorithms to extract surface reflection characteristics and quantify signal-to-noise behavior in low-SNR environments.

My work involves analyzing the raw data and applying coherent and incoherent integration techniques to isolate weak surface reflections from noise and system effects. By varying integration strategies and accumulation lengths, I am evaluating how reflected signal strength evolves with processing time, enabling robust estimation of reflection detectability and stability. This research bridges raw signal processing with physical interpretation of cryospheric surface properties and is contributing to an upcoming publication. Through this work, I am gaining experience in experimental GNSS-R analysis, algorithm development, and research-level technical communication.

Research Workflow

Raw Data Confirmation (probedata.m)

I am currently validating each acquisition day by inspecting the raw USRP binary recordings before performing any higher-level GNSS-R processing. Using a MATLAB workflow (probedata.m), I generate a compact set of diagnostics — a time-domain trace, a Welch power spectral density estimate, and a histogram of sample values — to confirm signal integrity, identify noise-only captures, and flag configuration or hardware-related irregularities.

This step allows me to directly compare field recordings against lab-collected noise references and use both spectral signatures and on-site notes to justify data screening decisions (including the exclusion of identified noise-only segments) before downstream analysis.

SNR Processing (Coherent & Incoherent Integration)

After raw-data confirmation, I am currently quantifying reflected-signal strength through signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) processing using coherent and incoherent integration techniques. By systematically varying coherent accumulation lengths and applying incoherent averaging, I evaluate how detectability improves with integration time while accounting for signal decorrelation and measurement variability across collection periods.

This SNR workflow provides a consistent, repeatable basis for comparing reflection quality across sites and days, and it supports the interpretation of reflected GNSS signal behavior over glacial surfaces for inclusion in the upcoming publication.
